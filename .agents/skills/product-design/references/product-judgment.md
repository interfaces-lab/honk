# Product judgment

## Decision order

1. The user's explicit goal and constraints.
2. Verified product behavior and system truth.
3. Repository `AGENTS.md`, owning component/platform guidance, and shared `packages/ui` contracts.
4. Accepted rules in this skill, then relevant shipped evidence.

Preserve the user's mental model and context unless changing it solves a verified problem. Name the
object, scope, and consequence of important actions. Prefer direct behavior and strong defaults over
new configuration. Use navigation for navigation and actions for actions. Match persistence to
importance; disclose inline before reaching for a modal.

Honk serves builders and engineers over one information architecture. The engineering-details dial may
change vocabulary and chrome density (`Uncommitted`/`Latest changes`, `Commit & Push`/`Ship it`,
`worktree`/`safe copy`, `merged`/`shipped`), but never capability or status meaning.
